Indiana University

Open Access Publishing Manager, Scholarly Communication, IUB Libraries

My work focuses on finding useful bridges between the humanities and information technology. I have a Ph.D. in English and began to bring network and machine technologies into my teaching and research when such methods were still known as Humanities Computing. In course work at ILS I have focused on data analysis and modeling, preservation and repositories, digital publishing, and web programming. Specific skills include: DSpace, R/RStudio, PHP, XSLT/XTF, Gephi, Ruby on Rails, Python, Jena/SPARQL, MySQL, d3 & gefx-js, Omeka, and Bootstrap front-end framework.

Education

Data interaction and visualization, computational research in the humanities, historical and literary text mining, semantic data, digital preservation, open access, web programming, networked cognition

Ph.D., English (2009)

18th- and 19th-century British literature, Victorian Studies, social mobility, narratives of affect and sentiment, histories of science and technology, textualities and readership networks

Two million views

YouTube channel Affective Ideologeme. Four years ago I uploaded two full BBC documentaries to YouTube, split into ten-minute chunks. These multipart documentaries, 1900s House and Regency House Party, were produced to highlight how life might have been lived in the past. I originally posted them for my students in literary studies. They've now been viewed over two million times.
