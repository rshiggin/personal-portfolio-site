Indiana University

Scholarly Communication Department, IU Libraries

I support the IUScholarWorks Institutional Repository (DSpace) and Open Access Journal program (Open Journal Systems).

My background and training have provided me with a skill set for building bridges between scholarly research and computational technologies. I have a PhD in English and began to bring network and machine technologies into my teaching and research when such methods were still known as Humanities Computing. After moving full time into the domain of Information and Library Science, I have worked in core areas of research enterprise environments, building expertise in scholarly communication, data management, and digital publishing, while also developing and managing the upgrade paths of several complex, library-based web applications.

Project Portfolio

New Drupal-based website for IUScholarWorks, centralizing services for self-submission to the institutional repository, copyright assessment, and open access support for scholarly research. $10,000 grant approved to customize and renovate the XTF platform application for Digital Humanities Conference Abstracts (ADHO). Omeka exhibit featuring 20+ hours of oral history interviews with William Sewell, a sociologist and former Chancellor of the University of Wisconsin. Data visualization project using D3.js for the Field Museum of Natural History in Chicago. Initial dataset for the Comic Book Readership Archive (CoBRA). "Visualizing Digital Humanities Activity at Indiana University," a network graph originally produced for UITS's Cyberinfrastructure for Digital Humanities. Final report for an LOD SPARQL semantic search tool programmed using a Java-based Jena endpoint, with a Fuseki web server and a unique OWL ontology derived from Alan Moore’s Watchmen.
